Weekly Recap: July 28

Author: Andrew Jang

Published on: July 231, 2023

Kiwoom stops their losing streak

After a loss in which they were shut out, Kiwoom would take the next two games against Lotte. Both teams debuted their new foreign players and saw them succeed, with Niko Goodrum making stellar defensive plays and Ronnie Dawson cranking a home run in only his second game. In the first game, it was all Charlie Barnes and the Lotte Bullpen with the Heroes being unable to score given any chance. However, in the next two games, Kiwoom scored big in the first couple of innings and relied on their pitching to secure them the victory.

Notable Performers: Charlie Barnes: 5.2 IP, 5 H, 5 BB, 2 K, W. Jeon Jun-Woo: 2 for 8, 2 R, 2 HR, 4 RBI. Kim Min-Suk: 6 for 11, 1 RBI. Ronnie Dawson: 4 for 8, 4 R, 1 HR, 3 RBI. Song Sung-Moon: 3 for 12, 4 RBI. Lim Chang-Min: 2 IP, 2 SV, 2 K.

Doosan extends their win streak to 10

Hur Kyoung-Min and José Rojas home runs as well as a Park Jun-Young 3 RBI Triple helped secure the win for the Bears over the Tigers. Rain delays suspended the rest of their series, but Doosan will look to maintain their big win streak in their upcoming series vs Lotte.

Notable Performers: José Rojas: 1 for 2, 2 R, 1 HR, 1 RBI. Park Jun-Young: 1 for 3, 3 RBI. Raúl Alcántara: 6 IP, 6 H, 2 BB, 2 K, 1 R. Socrates Brito: 2 for 4, 2 R, 1 HR, 1 RBI. Park Chan-Ho: 2 for 4. Mario Sanchez: 6.2 IP, 4 H, 2 HR, 1 BB, 10 K, 4 R.

KT closes in on a playoff spot

KT took two out of the three games vs Samsung and moved up to the sixth place. After their recent victory, they stand only 0.5 games behind Lotte for 5th place. KT's Offense propelled them to score 17 runs in the three games with notable performances from Kim Min-Hyuk, Bae Jung-Dae, and Eom Sang-Baek. Samsung was able to win one game by scoring three runs in the seventh of game two. Additionally, Kang Min-Ho's home run in the second inning of the same game made him the catcher with the most home runs of all time.

Notable Performers: Jose Pirela: 6 for 11, 1 R, 2 RBI. Kang Min-Ho: 3 for 11, 2 R, 1 HR, 3 RBI. Won Tae-In: 6.1 IP, 6 H, 1 BB, 8 K, 2 R. Eom Sang-Baek: 6.2 IP, 5 H, 1 HR, 2 BB, 4 K, 2 R. Kim Min-Hyuk: 7 for 14, 2 R, 5 RBI. Bae Jung-Dae: 4 for 10, 3 R, 3 RBI.

Dinos sweep Hanhwa in shortened series

Home runs from Son Ah-Seop, Park Gun-Woo, and Yoon Hyung-Joon helped NC secure the win over the Eagles. Two more homeruns from Jason Martin fueled a late game comeback to take game two against Hanhwa.

Notable Performers: Jason Martin: 3 for 10, 2 R, 2 HR, 4 RBI. Son Ah-Seop: 5 for 8, 3 R, 1 HR, 2 RBI. Erick Fedde: 6.2 IP, 4 H, 1 BB, 8 K, 3 R. Jung Eun-Won: 3 for 6, 2 R, 1 HR, 3 RBI. Lee Do-Yoon: 2 for 5, 2 R. Felix Pena: 6 IP, 5 H, 1 HR, 2 BB, 7 K, 3 R.

SSG wins against LG in clash of 1st and 2nd place.

SSG wins 6-4 in a series with LG which was rained out. Great performances from SSG's lineup as well as their bullpen helped them secure the win.

Notable Performers: Choi Jeong: 3 for 4, 1 R, 2 RBI. Choi Joo-Hwan: 1 for 5, 1 R, 1 HR, 2 RBI. Kim Kwang-Hyun: 6 IP, 7 H, 2 BB, 2K, 4 R. Hong Chang-Ki: 2 for 5, 2 R. Austin Dean: 2 for 4 , 2 RBI. Casey Kelly: 5 IP, 6 H, 1 HR, 2 BB, 4 K, 5 R.
