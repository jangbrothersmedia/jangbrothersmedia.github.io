Hitters

Doosan's attack has been at the forefront of their title runs over the past decade. Led by 2018 MVP Jae-Hwan Kim and a handful of Korea's finest contact hitters, Doosan consistently has the strongest lineup in the KBO.

Catchers: Sei-Hyuk Park(#10), Seung-Hyun Jang(#22)

Infielders: Jose Fernandez(#9), Jae-Il Oh(#36), Jae-Won Oh(#24), Joo-Hwan Choi(#53), Jae-Ho Kim(#52), Kyeong-Min Heo(#13), Yoo-Chan Lee(#14)

Outfielders: Jae-Hwan Kim(#32), Kun-Woo Park(#37), Soo-Bin Jung(#31), Su-Haeng Cho(#8)

AVG: .293

HR: 125

OPS: .792

Pitchers

Backing up Doosan's top tier attack is the steady pitching hands produced by the team's farm system. In a league plagued by inconsistency in the pitching department, Doosan has been one of the few consistent pitching staffs in the KBO. Doosan pairs their power pitching with a bullpen reliant on finesse to put up one of the best statlines in the league.

Starters: Won-Jun Choi(#61), Chris Flexen(#34), Raul Alcantara(#43), Hui-Guan Yoo(#29), Duk-Joo Ham(#1)

Relievers: Gon-Hui Hong(#17), Seung-Jeen Lee(#55), Kang-Ryul Kim(#27), Hyung-Beom Lee(#68)

Closers: Myeong-Jun Yoon(#42), Young-Ha Lee(#50))

ERA: 4.31

SO: 1046

WHIP: 1.46

Coaches

Doosan's decisions are made by manager Tae-Hyeong Kim, who has also been the manager of the Korean National Team due to his success in the managerial position. He is aided by a bevvy of assistant coaches, mostly former players who look to continue their involvement in the KBO.

Manager: Tae-Hyeong Kim

Hitting Coach: Dong-Woo Kang

Bullpen Coach: Jae-Hoon Jung

Defense Coach: Sung-Hwan Cho

Battery Coach: In-Sung Cho
